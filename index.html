My Favourite Movies

  • Prison Break Prison Break follows a man, Michael Scofield, on his mission to break his brother out of prison. Michael is convinced that his brother, Lincoln Burrows, is innocent of the crime for which he was committed, which was the murder of the vice president’s brother. Burrows is on death row, and scheduled for execution within the month. Scofield must make connections on the prison block that will aid him in his escape, but enemies are sure to make slow work of Michael’s plans.
  • Blacklist The Blacklist is an American crime drama television series. It premiered on NBC in 2013, and follows the life of former U.S. Navy officer turned high-profile criminal, Raymond "Red" Reddington (played by James Spader), who voluntarily surrenders to the FBI after eluding capture for decades. He tells the FBI that he has a list of the most dangerous criminals in the world that he has compiled over his criminal career, and is willing to inform on their operations in exchange for immunity from prosecution. The series follows Red as he works with an FBI profiler, Elizabeth Keen (played by Megan Boone), to track down and eliminate criminals from his "blacklist".
  • Money Heist Money Heist is a Spanish television series created by Álex Pina. It originally aired on Antena 3 in Spain, and later on Netflix internationally. The show is a crime thriller that follows a mysterious man known as "The Professor" who gathers a group of eight people with unique skills to carry out the biggest heist in Spanish history, by holding hostages and threatening to blow up the Royal Mint of Spain. The series follows the planning, execution, and aftermath of the heist, as the robbers face unexpected challenges and police attempt to rescue the hostages and capture the robbers. The show has received critical acclaim for its complex characters, intricate plotting, and high-stakes suspense.
  • 24 Legacy 24: Legacy is an American television series that is a spin-off of the popular show 24. It premiered on Fox in 2017 and stars Corey Hawkins as Eric Carter, a former Army Ranger who is pulled back into action when his comrades from a mission in Yemen become targets of a terrorist group. With the help of former CTU (Counter Terrorist Unit) head Rebecca Ingram (played by Miranda Otto), Eric must stop the terrorists and protect his own family. The show follows the same real-time format as its predecessor, with each episode covering one hour in a day of the crisis. 24: Legacy explores themes of terrorism, national security, and the personal lives of the characters as they work to prevent a large-scale attack on American soil.
  • 24 Seasons 24 is an American television series that originally aired on Fox from 2001 to 2010, with a follow-up limited series in 2014. The show is a real-time action and drama series that follows the exploits of federal agent Jack Bauer (played by Kiefer Sutherland), who works for the Counter Terrorist Unit (CTU) and is tasked with preventing terrorist attacks on American soil. Each season of 24 covers a single day in the life of Jack Bauer, with each episode representing one hour in real time. The show is known for its non-stop action, complex characters, and twist-filled storylines, and was highly acclaimed for its innovative format and edge-of-your-seat suspense.
  • The Catch The Catch is an American television comedy-drama series that originally aired on ABC from 2016 to 2017. It stars Mireille Enos as Alice Vaughan, a private investigator who is about to get married, but gets left at the altar. The series then follows her pursuit of her fiancé, Benjamin Jones (played by Peter Krause), who is actually a con artist working for a high-end fraud organization. The show balances humor and drama as Alice tries to uncover the truth about Benjamin and take down his criminal network.
  • What Happened To Monday What Happened to Monday is a science fiction movie released in 2017. It is set in a future where the world is overpopulated, and the government has implemented a strict "One Child Policy," in which families are only allowed to have one child. The film follows a set of identical septuplets named after the days of the week, who were raised in secret and live their lives as a single person, taking turns assuming the identity of "Karen Settman" in order to avoid detection. When Monday goes missing, the remaining sisters must work together to uncover the truth behind her disappearance and protect their family, while also navigating the political and social issues of the dystopian world they live in.
  • Peppermint Peppermint is a 2018 American vigilante action thriller film directed by Pierre Morel. The film stars Jennifer Garner as Riley North, a mother who seeks revenge against the drug cartel responsible for the deaths of her husband and daughter. After being in a coma for five years following the attack, Riley awakens and embarks on a mission to bring justice to those responsible. The film follows her journey as she becomes a one-woman army, using her skills and determination to take down the criminal organization and anyone who stands in her way. Peppermint explores themes of grief, justice, and vigilantism, and is known for its high-octane action scenes and powerful central performance by Jennifer Garner.
  • The Intruder The Intruder is a 2019 American psychological thriller film directed by Deon Taylor. The film stars Michael Ealy and Meagan Good as a young couple who purchase a beautiful Napa Valley home from a charming older man named Charlie Peck (played by Dennis Quaid), only to find out that he is not willing to let go of the property. As Charlie's behavior becomes increasingly erratic and dangerous, the couple is forced to defend themselves and their home from his aggressive advances. The Intruder explores themes of obsession, trust, and the danger of letting someone into your life and home, and is known for its suspenseful plot and strong performances by its lead actors.
